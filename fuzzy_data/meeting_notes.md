# Intake call — Senior Backend Engineer search
**Recruiter:** Dana (agency)  ·  **Client hiring manager:** Marcus (VP Eng, a Series-B fintech)  ·  **Duration:** ~28 min (condensed transcript)

---

**Dana:** Thanks for making time, Marcus. Let's scope the backend role. What does the ideal hire look like?

**Marcus:** Sure. Top line: senior backend, someone who can own our payments service end to end. Go or Rust ideally. We're on Go today but honestly if someone's brilliant in Rust I wouldn't rule them out.

**Dana:** Got it. Years of experience you're anchoring on?

**Marcus:** Call it seven-plus. But — look, I'll be honest, the "years" number matters less to me than whether they've actually run something at scale. We had a staff engineer last year with twelve years on paper who'd never touched a real on-call rotation. That was painful.

**Dana:** Noted. Compensation band?

**Marcus:** Base up to 210, and there's equity on top. We can flex a little for the right person, maybe 220 if they're exceptional, but I'd rather not advertise that.

**Dana:** And location? Remote, hybrid, onsite?

**Marcus:** We're "hybrid." Officially two days a week in the NYC office.

**Dana:** Officially?

**Marcus:** [laughs] Yeah. Between us — the team is basically remote-first in practice. The two-days thing is a policy on paper that leadership likes to point to. Nobody's counting badge swipes. If a candidate is genuinely great and fully remote, I can make it work, I just can't say that in the job post.

**Dana:** Understood, I'll read between the lines there. What about the must-have skills?

**Marcus:** Distributed systems fundamentals. Real database chops — we've got some gnarly Postgres stuff. Event-driven architecture, they should be comfortable with Kafka or similar. And — this is important — I want someone who's opinionated but not dogmatic. I've been burned by brilliant people who can't disagree without turning it into a holy war.

**Dana:** That's more of a working-style thing. How do you weigh that against raw technical ability?

**Marcus:** Honestly? For this hire the collaboration piece might matter more. The team's a little fragile right now — we lost two people in Q3 and morale took a hit. I need someone who stabilizes the room, not someone who's technically god-tier but makes everyone else feel small. Though I'd never phrase it that way to the candidate.

**Dana:** That's really useful context. Anything on domain background — do they need fintech or payments experience?

**Marcus:** Nice to have, not required. I'd take a strong distributed-systems person from, say, gaming or logistics and teach them payments. The regulatory stuff we can onboard. What I can't teach is judgment under pressure.

**Dana:** Last thing — timeline and urgency?

**Marcus:** We'd love someone in seat by end of quarter. But I want to be careful — I'd rather wait for the right person than rush a mediocre one in. We did that in Q1 and it cost us. So: urgent, but not so urgent I'll lower the bar.

**Dana:** Perfect. I think I've got a clear picture. I'll send over a calibrated shortlist by Friday.

**Marcus:** Great. And Dana — the collaboration thing? Weight it heavily. I know it's hard to screen for, but it's the thing that'll actually make or break this hire.
